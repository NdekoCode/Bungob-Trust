How You Can Help us

How You Can Help We need your help to build a model for poverty alleviation and climate change adaptation that is sustainable, replicable and scalable. This model is founded on local resources and know-how. We have supported eight (8) groups – totalling 105 members (from 105 households) in seven villages by providing micro loans, farm inputs and training in the past year. Some of the groups have harvested their farm produce once and are preparing for their second harvest in July/August 2014.

The Bongob Trust dream of enabling community members

  • Earn an income to meet their basic household needs
  • Support some of the needs of the most vulnerable (orphans, elderly and disabled)
  • Lead communities improve their public health needs (improved access to water and sanitation)
  • Promote social cohesion and peace

Is beginning to become reality, as seven groups are beginning to reap benefit from our poverty alleviation model and 21 orphans are able to go to school. There is a need to support more groups so that more people can earn an income There is a need to scale up production to meet the demand for some of the outputs

However, we are currently only able to reach a small fraction of the 240,000 people living in different communities in the Momo area because of our limited resources.

We are appealing to aid agencies, private sector corporate bodies and individuals of good will to support us in partnership and help so that we expand and deepen the scope of this programme. You may wish to ear mark your donation to Bongob Trust for a specific project to ensure your money goes where it is intended.

Our current projects include:

Support for the most disadvantaged
Promotion of community peace
Promote actions that generate journals
Fight poverty
Environmental protection.

Funds donated to Bongob Trust will be used effectively to achieve the goals of sustainable development for the people of Momo area and eventually beyond to reduce and alleviate poverty, increase social inclusion, and promote social justice. All funding and expenditure is transparent and accountable through audited accounts.
