Harouna (L) & Frankline (R)

Since starting in September 2011 we have set up an office in Mbengwi, registered as a non-profit organisation, and have hired an Agronomist, Frankline Angwe Nguh as the Bongob project co-ordinator. We are supporting 07 co-operative projects and 01 individual enterprise and which are on-going. These projects are being realised on land allotted by groups and approved by traditional authorities and endorsed by the local administration.

The projects include: pig rearing, maize, bean, cocoyam, plantain growing and chicken farming. The 08 projects have a total of 105 people involved in 7 different villages. The groups implementing have been supported with micro loans, tools and free agricultural and technical training, advice and assistance from us in collaboration with local specialists.

We have opened dialogue to settle once and for all the long standing conflict between grazers and farmers that costs on average 6 lives every year in the region. Moreover, we have introduced and are promoting a new practical solution (Sisal fencing) to the conflict that has marred every development effort in many regions of the country for over seven decades.

We have sought the authoritative collaboration of the traditional rulers to sensitise and encourage the planting of sisal plant in order to settle once and for all the long standing conflict between graziers and farmers.

We are building partnership with local institutions to improve and strengthen our action.
