The challenges and the main beneficiaries of our action

Farmers

Momo area is typified by individual farmers producing for their own needs with a little left over for sale at the local market. This hand to mouth economy leaves little for health and education which must be paid for, and nothing for growth and wealth creation.

Unemployment is high in the region with no economic growth creating jobs. Social structures are breaking down with most young people leaving the rural areas for the cities. With no money in their pockets, access to health facilities is far-fetched. And typhoid, Diarrhoea and malaria is claiming more and more lives in the villages.

Villagers

The wide use of simple, insecure and poorly designed pit latrines and unprotected water sources account for over 50% of hospital admissions (Acha Hospital, 2011). Our action aims to enable villagers earn an income and so are able to improve their access to good sanitation and water facilities.

Orphans

HIV/Aids is a major problem and has left many orphans in the area. These orphans are supported by their extended family and the community and are a growing burden on their already meagre resources.

The Disabled and the Elderly

The villages are losing young people to the cities leaving the old and disabled people unable to support themselves.

By providing sustainable development the project aims to regenerate local employment stemming the movement away from the villages.
