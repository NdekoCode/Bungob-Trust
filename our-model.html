Our Model

Economic strengthening of groups of people and their households, thereby, giving them the means and opportunity to lift their communities as a whole out of poverty.

We identify local production groups within communities

  • Empower them by supporting them to develop their capacities and resources.
  • The expectation is to see the life of each and every member improved.

We believe in local production groups

We believe that a group that can improve the lives of its members (and therefore their households) can confidently influence the development policies and trends of their community. We therefore provide support to struggling community groups in a way that.

will promote sustainable agricultural and business practice, with the happy consequence to

  • Motivate collective support to the destitute (orphans, elderly and disabled) as well as.
  • inspire communal love and action for community wellbeing.

We realise that for our Model to succeed in the fight to overcome poverty and suffering, we have to select the cooperative groups (our local partners) carefully, strengthen their structure, trained them diligently and guide them to become autonomous building the sustainability on their cultural fabrics.

Our action starts in Momo division, arguably the least developed area in the North West Region of Cameroon, from where, in a couple of years a tried and tested model would be encouraged to be applied in other geographical locations taking care to adapt it to the existing local culture.

The strength of the model is embedded in the process of building up the cooperative group.

The first step consist of identifying existing self-help groups (men, women or mixed). People who get into groups for production purposes already recognise the fact that there is strength in numbers. Working together makes it possible for them to reduce the burden of work on individual shoulders, improve yield as well as enabling them to economically interact with markets. We identify them and their common initiative concept.

The second step is farm education and training for the identified group by qualified and skilled field officers. We take the latest practices from top academic agronomists and local success stories and translate that into simple, easy-to-understand lessons.

These are delivered in the national language best understood by the beneficiaries. We encourage cross learning among the groups from different communities.

The third step consists of providing the capital - environmentally-sensitive planting materials, equipment and manure. Our manure (fertilizer only when very necessary) provides badly-needed nutrients to soils that have been stripped bare over decades of poor management.

The fourth step is post-harvest handling and market facilitation. Our field officers provide extensive training and storage options, so that farmers do not experience post-harvest crop loss.

When farmers can store safely, they can access the markets several months after harvest, when prices are higher. We even connect the potential buyers in the wider market to the groups and their products.

The final step in the process is for the group to join the BT farmer’s cooperative union. We intend to forge the production groups in a couple of years into a powerful local cooperative capable of negotiating market prices and being more resilient to economic shocks

We take a group from formation through production, harvest, marketing, and savings. We open avenues to encourage group work and motivation for community development as the responsibility of all.

We believe that a community that succeeds with this model will become tomorrow’s examples of people able to remove themselves out of the depths of poverty as well as inspiring entire communities to step up their own coordinated development efforts.
